02 — THE STORY

I wanted to understand how the products I was valuing were actually built.

From 2017 I advised companies across the knowledge economy — boardrooms, models, term sheets. By 2022 I'd gravitated to pure tech investment banking, spending my days with the founders and teams building real products. That proximity became a problem, in the best way: advising on the deals stopped being enough.

So I learned to build. Nights and weekends first, then fully — in 2025 I made the switch to engineering. What I kept from banking is the judgment: how to scope what's worth building, communicate clearly with stakeholders, and treat every project like it has a P&L.

I manage my own investment portfolio, I like keeping both mind and life in motion, and I care most about work at the intersection of tech and finance — where both halves of my career compound.
