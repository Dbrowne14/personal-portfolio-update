# Creative Direction

## Central idea

From evaluating products to shipping them.

This is not primarily a portfolio about changing careers. It is about moving from evaluating technology businesses and products to designing, building and shipping products directly.

Every major page, section, transition and interaction should reinforce this idea.

## Positioning

David Browne is a London-based frontend-leaning full-stack developer with approximately eight years of commercial experience in technology investment banking.

The portfolio should position him as:

- A thoughtful product-minded engineer
- A strong frontend practitioner
- Someone with commercial judgment and stakeholder experience
- Someone capable of owning and shipping complete products
- A candidate for frontend and full-stack engineering roles

It should not position him primarily as:

- A freelance developer
- An agency
- A junior developer documenting a learning journey
- A creative coder producing effects without purpose

## Audience

Primary:

- Engineering managers
- Technical recruiters
- Product-led technology companies
- Design-led engineering teams
- Founders and product leaders

Secondary:

- Potential collaborators
- Other developers and designers
- Occasional suitable freelance opportunities

## Desired impression

The visitor should remember:

"David spent years evaluating technology products. Now he ships them."

The site should feel authored, intelligent, technically ambitious and professionally credible.

## Visual character

- Light-first
- Predominantly white editorial canvas
- Strong black typography
- Confident accent colour used as foreground
- Architectural composition
- Large differences in typographic scale
- High-quality project imagery
- Precise grid and spacing
- Small UI-inspired injections of colour and data
- One major signature interaction supported by restrained motion

Avoid:

- Generic developer dashboards
- Large technology-logo walls
- Skill progress bars
- Neon cyberpunk styling
- Spotify-coded green and black
- Large grey section backgrounds
- Excessive cream or concrete-grey panels
- Generic rounded SaaS cards
- Agency sales language
- Scroll hijacking
- Decorative motion with no narrative function

## Narrative structure

1. Introduction: who David is and the central proposition
2. Transition: evaluating products becomes shipping products
3. Proof: selected products and commercial work
4. Context: experience, judgment and technical capabilities
5. Contact: a professional invitation to continue the conversation

## Motion principle

Motion should express the transition from analysis to creation.

The opening experience may begin structured, measured and static. As the visitor progresses, the layout and interactions may become more expressive, fluid and product-like.

Every animation should answer at least one of these questions:

- Does it reveal information?
- Does it show progression?
- Does it connect two parts of the narrative?
- Does it improve spatial understanding?
- Does it make an interaction feel more direct?

If not, it should not be included.

## Signature interaction

The preferred direction is for the hero typography or graphic architecture to transform into the career/product transition.

Possible expression:

- The hero begins with monumental typography.
- A rule, line, letterform or axis emerges from the hero.
- That element develops into the transition timeline.
- The timeline moves from financial-analysis visual language into product-delivery visual language.
- It should feel like one continuous experience rather than two unrelated sections.

The implementation must have:

- A strong non-animated first frame
- A reduced-motion alternative
- A simplified mobile version
- No scroll locking
- No dependence on excessive JavaScript
